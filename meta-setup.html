Set up your Meta ad account — 10 minutes, once, ever.

Your ads run on an ad account YOU own — full transparency, and every dollar of ad spend goes straight from your card to Meta. Here's the whole setup, then one click to connect us.

1

Create your Meta Business Portfolio

This is the free "business container" Facebook and Instagram ads live inside.

  1. Go to business.facebook.com and log in with the Facebook account that manages (or will manage) your clinic's Facebook page.
  2. Click Create account → enter your clinic's name, your name, and your business email.
  3. Confirm the email Meta sends you.
✓ Already have Business Manager for your clinic? Skip to Step 2.
2

Create your ad account

  1. Inside Business Manager, open Settings (gear icon) → Accounts → Ad accounts.
  2. Click Add → Create a new ad account.
  3. Name it after your clinic (e.g., "Smith Chiropractic Ads"), set your time zone and USD, and assign yourself as the person who manages it.
✓ Already run (or ran) any Facebook ads? You likely have an ad account — find it in the same menu and skip to Step 3.
3

Add your payment method

This is the card your ad budget charges to — it goes directly to Meta, never through us.

  1. In Business Manager: Settings → Billing & payments → Payment methods → Add.
  2. Add the business card you want funding your ads (plan on a minimum of $50/day).
4

Make sure your clinic's Facebook page is connected

Ads run "from" your clinic's Facebook page. In Settings → Accounts → Pages, confirm your page is listed — if not, click Add → Add a page and claim it.

Final step — the one everyone misses

This button securely grants our fulfillment team access to run ads in the account you just set up. It takes about 30 seconds: log in with the same Facebook account, review what's being shared, click Approve. You can revoke access anytime — the account stays 100% yours.

Stuck? Two common snags: ① Meta asks you to "verify your business" — usually not required for this; if it blocks you, tell us and we'll walk it together on your onboarding call. ② You had an old ad account that's disabled — don't fight it; create a fresh one under your new Business Portfolio (Step 2) and connect that instead. Anything else: reply to your onboarding email or text your account manager — that's what we're for.
